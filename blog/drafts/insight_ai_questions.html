Insight AI application questionare

Date Wed 11 January 2017

Describe your research/and or your professional experience to a non-expert.

From 2003 to 2005, I had worked as a system programmer on various projects, including building a query recommendation system for a web search engine [1] and developing an operating system for an atomic force microscope electronic controller system [2]. During my graduate study and postdoctroal research, I have worked on topics at the intersection of physics, mathematics, and computational science. Throughout my research career I have written several papers [3] and have given talks [4] at various places about the study of quantum field theories and their non-perturbative aspects via string theory, as well as have developed a couple of python programs [5] with web interface.

What is the latest deep learning innovation that really excites you?

self-driving car relation to physics

What excites you about becoming an AI professional and working in the tech industry?

I have always been intrigued by challenging research topics that require scientific methods, mathematical ideas, and computational analyses, and I find machine learning to be a perfect fit to the description.

During my research career as a string theorist, it has been frustrating for me to suffer from the lack of data to test various physical and mathematical ideas on, and also to work against the lukewarn acceptance of the string theory community toward computational analysis.

I find researches in machine learning to be the opposite of my research experience in string theory: there is ample data upon which to build and test a mathematical model of a real-world phenomenon.

During my professional career as a system programmer, I enjoyed working at the interface of software and hardware, and the fact that the advancement of machine learning requires good understanding of hardware systems to operate training and inference appeals to me.
