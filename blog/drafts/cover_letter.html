Cover Letter

Date Wed 11 January 2017

I am a postdoctoral fellow at the physics department of Rutgers University in New Jersey studying string theory. I respectfully submit my application for the Google Brain Residency 2017 Program, as the program will be a great place for my transition from a theoretical physicist to a deep learning researcher.

I have both professional and academic research experiences. From 2003 to 2005, I had worked as a system programmer on various projects, including building a query recommendation system for a web search engine [1] and developing an operating system for an atomic force microscope electronic controller system [2]. During my graduate study and postdoctoral research, I have worked on topics at the intersection of physics, mathematics, and computational science. Throughout my research career I have written several papers [3] and have given talks [4] at various places about the study of quantum field theories and their non-perturbative aspects via string theory, as well as have developed a couple of python programs [5] with web interface, about which I gave a talk at SciPy 2016 conference [6]. Although I do not have a research experience in deep learning, I hope these experiences have prepared me to start a new research career in deep learning.

I have always been intrigued by challenging research topics that require scientific methods, mathematical ideas, and computational analyses, and I find machine learning to be a perfect fit to the description: nowadays there is ample data upon which to build and test a mathematical model of a real-world phenomenon, and that is what excited me about becoming a deep learning researcher. In addition, I enjoyed working at the interface of software and hardware when I was a system programmer during my professional career, and the fact that the advancement of deep learning requires good understanding of hardware systems to design and operate deep learning software appeals greatly to me.

I am interested in various aspects of deep learning. Regarding theoretical side of deep learning, I want to study how the physics of renormalization group can guide us in estimating the capacity of a deep convolutional network and vice versa, extending previous studies [7] and analyzing them with examples. For a practical question in deep learning, I find the problem described in Jeff Dean's talk at Montreal deep learning school in 2016 [8] about using reinforcement learning to place nodes of a calculation graph of a deep learning model at suitable devices in a distributed system very interesting. Lastly, developing autonomous vehicle systems is a very appealing project to me, because it will not only involve real-time audiovisual recognition and processing, which is the frontier of the current deep learning research, but also require the software to be organically integrated with the hardware of a passenger vehicle.

I have a wide range of background and interest from string theory to kernel programming and feel comfortable with communicating both in mathematics and in source codes. I am certain that this will contribute to the diversity of the Google Residency Program and also that I will be able to learn a great amount from wonderful members at Google Brain during the residency.

Thank you very much for your consideration of my application.
